Shortline Freight Sandbox

Subtraction

A browser game about managing a brittle regional railroad where every monthly decision trades future stability for short-term relief.

Launch Game Read Full Notes

Origin

Subtraction started as a strategic decline simulator and evolved into a monthly freight operations game with branch-level economics, debt pressure, market shifts, and customer failure risk. The tone is intentionally dark: you can try to run a responsible recovery, or optimize extraction before collapse.

How To Play

  1. Open the game and review the baseline month already generated for reference.
  2. Pick one primary action each month (branch work or corporate policy).
  3. Set the squeeze amount in the slush panel, then arm the action as an additional side action.
  4. Set the five slush allocation percentages so they add to exactly 100 before arming.
  5. Watch the dashboards for moved carloads, locomotive capacity, branch profit, service capacity, and covenant runway.
  6. Stay above covenant limits and avoid sustained traffic collapse if you want to survive.

Dashboard Guide

  • Company Snapshot: the top-line health check. It shows cash, slush fund, debt, locomotive capacity, service capacity, morale, confidence, and covenant runway for each month.
  • Company Cost Ledger: the monthly expense breakdown. Use it to see what payroll, locomotive leases, debt service, infrastructure, disruption, and policy choices actually cost you.
  • External Factors: the market view. Each branch cell shows shipper cars / receiver cars and that lane's status so you can see demand pressure and customer health at a glance.
  • Branch Infrastructure Ledger: the branch-by-branch scorecard. It shows each line's status, revenue, cost, profit, margin, and the profit ranking for the month.
  • Monthly Dispatcher Report: the narrative summary of what just happened, including action effects, incidents, customer failures, and any employee consequences.
  • Game Log: the running audit trail. It keeps the compact month-by-month event history so you can review what led to the current state.
  • Add to Slush Fund: the side action panel. It tracks personal profit and pleasure projects, their funding progress, the separate CEO lifestyle score, and the squeeze amount you’re about to arm.

Strategy Hints

  • Use the baseline month to identify which branches are carrying margin and which are structural drags.
  • Track condition decay is staged: neglect at low condition causes much faster deterioration.
  • Maintenance and repair now grant temporary grace periods; stagger work to smooth decay over time.
  • Leased locomotives are expensive, but they increase monthly locomotive capacity and can relieve throughput bottlenecks.
  • The Return Leased Locomotives action only appears when you currently have leased units in service.
  • Confidence and morale matter indirectly through service quality and moved-car conversion, and low morale now can trigger burnout, quits, sabotage, unionization, or whistleblowing.
  • Slush projects unlock at $10k, $50k, $100k, $200k, and $400k funding and trigger morale-hitting party events.
  • Renaming the road is possible, but branding campaigns cost cash and hit your cushion.
